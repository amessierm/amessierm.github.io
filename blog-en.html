Ski from Kuujjuaq to George-River winter camping

Published april 23rd 2024

We're back from our cross-country skiing expedition between Kuujjuaq and George-River in Nunavik from March 29 to April 6. The expedition lasted a total of 8 days, with the first day spent on snowmobile transport. The route followed the snowmobile trail between the two villages, heading northeast. We started skiing from the Whale River, then crossed the Marralik, Tuttutuuq, Qurlutuq and finally George rivers. The mild temperatures of the week before our departure made us worry that the rivers and lakes wouldn't be frozen enough for the crossings, but in the end everything was good and frosty. We enjoyed comfortable temperatures below freezing, around -5°C.

Our only weather-related problem occurred on days 1 and 2. A blizzard of wet snow hit us after only 10km of our crossing. We had to cut short our first real day of skiing to wait for the blizzard to pass and visibility to improve. Wet snow is perhaps worse than rain! Finally, on day 2, we were able to cover the distances required for our traverse. A total of around 150 km of skiing. We didn't come across anyone else until the sixth day of skiing!

Thanks to Charlotte Perret who completed the expedition last year for her advice, and to Philippe Poulin for the cartography.

Do we recommend it? Yes! Would we do it again? Certainly! And for even longer. Anyone with time could take a break once in George-River and continue on the Koroc River to Torngats Park.

After a polar bear visited the village of Kuujjuaq this winter, we wanted to step up our bear precautions. We tried going out with a dog, but finally opted for a fence (less food to lug around!). I built it with aluminum angles (fairly light) and trip-wire alarms. Installation was rather tedious at first, given the knotted fishing wire, but I refined the system by using wooden sticks to wind the ropes. Another problem was that we had a few days of freezing rain, which coated the alarm mechanisms with ice. I had to install a makeshift raincoat made from plastic bags to protect them. I was also glad to have brought some lock antifreeze to de-ice the mechanisms.
